Liverpool should take up Manchester United transfer ‘offer’ to fix FSG problem in the ideal way

FSG don’t have too many problems to fix in the summer transfer window, but they could solve one weak point by muscling in on a Manchester United ‘offer’.

It won’t exactly be a quiet summer for FSG. Rumour has it that there are a few pretty important contracts to be resolved — this is bound to have a knock-on effect on transfer planning too. But as things stand, the Liverpool squad is as strong as it has ever been. Compared to his predecessor, Julian Ward will be playing on easy mode.

That being said, the best teams cannot afford to stand still, not if they want to go on to dominate an era. With Jürgen Klopp extending through to 2026, the goal now will be to build up a trophy haul that better reflects his Liverpool greatness. To do that, one or two problems in the squad could still benefit from being addressed. The solution could lie with Manchester United.

No, Ward won’t be plotting any audacious Old Trafford raids. Such is the plight of Manchester United at present, it is far from clear that Liverpool would want a single one of their players. The idea of moving for Paul Pogba on a free transfer is an interesting thought experiment, but little more than that. Klopp would perhaps fancy his chances of rescuing Jadon Sancho. Overall, though, there is far more astute business to be done elsewhere.

The upshot of this state of affairs is that Manchester United are very much in the market for new recruits. There is no doubt that Erik Ten Hag will need to get busy if he wants to make the side competitive. But Liverpool are quite obviously the more attractive destination — and FSG could muscle in on any transfer deal ‘offered’ to their rivals.

One of the many Manchester United weaknesses is at right-back. As with most of their other deficiencies, this is not for the want of spending — at £50m, Aaron Wan-Bissaka was meant to be the long-term fix. To the surprise of precisely zero Liverpool fans, however, comparisons to Trent Alexander-Arnold quickly proved to be not merely spurious but downright offensive.

As a result, when Ten Hag takes the helm, he could be tempted by a reunion with Sergiño Dest. Barcelona have reportedly ‘offered’ the former Ajax full-back to Manchester United, who has started just six games this year under Xavi. While Liverpool are not generally in the habit of helping the Catalans offload their rejects, there is plenty to suggest that he would make an ideal back-up to Alexander-Arnold.

Especially if Neco Williams makes his Anfield departure permanent this summer, a second-choice right-back will be a clear area for FSG to address. Joe Gomez has his strengths, and has improved the offensive side of his game, but there is no escaping the fact he is a centre-back by trade. Anyone hoping to properly deputise for Alexander-Arnold needs to have impeccable attacking instincts.

Dest brings this in abundance. Over the last year, his expected goals per game tally is in the 90th percentile for defenders in Europe’s top five leagues, and his expected assists place him in the 72nd percentile. His pass completion rate ranks in the 97th percentile, his progressive carries and dribbles both also sit in the 97th percentile, and he averages more than three touches per game in the opposition penalty area (putting him in the 93rd percentile). In short, he is extremely attack-minded.

The biggest concern is the relative lack of crosses into the box. Clearly, this is a major Alexander-Arnold trademark, and it is not something that Dest really offers. However, still only 21, he can certainly work on this. Likewise, at Liverpool, he would be part of a triangle with the likes of Mohamed Salah and Jordan Henderson or Naby Keïta. He would not always have to be the one delivering the final ball.

With Barcelona keen to offload, it is hard to imagine the transfer fee even reaching the £18.9m they initially paid to take Dest from Ajax. Any price below that is a major bargain for such a young, talented player. It is little wonder that Chelsea and Tottenham have joined Manchester United in taking 'more than a passing interest'. For FSG, there is the added marketability bonus of signing such a bright talent from the USA.

If FSG can box off this area of ‘weakness’ in the Liverpool squad, the only pressing thing left on the summer transfer to-do list will be to find an understudy for Fabinho in defensive midfield. With a little help from Manchester United, Ward’s first summer as sporting director might be about to get even easier.
